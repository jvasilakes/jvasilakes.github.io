About

Beginning September 2020, I will be a PhD student at the National Center for Text Mining (NaCTeM) at the University of Manchester, advised by Prof. Sophia Ananiadou. My research focus will be context-aware relationship extraction of the scientific literature.
Until then, I am research programmer in biomedical natural language processing at the Institute for Health Informatics at the University of Minnesota. Prior to this position, I worked on automatic speech recognition at the University of Cambridge.

I recieved my MS from the University of Edinburgh's Speech and Language Processing program. My thesis, supervised by Ewan Klein, involved adding an open-domain semantic parser to NLTK using CCG and Neo-Davidsonian event semantics.

I completed my BA in Philosophy at Loyola University of Chicago. My honors thesis was titled "The Word of Speech". In it I discussed the theory of speech introduced by Merleau-Ponty in which the speech act is analogous to bodily interaction with the world.

In my free time, I am an avid cook and cyclist.
