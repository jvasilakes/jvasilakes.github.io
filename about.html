About

In November I finished a a research assistantship under Dr. Mark Gales at the University of Cambridge on the IARPA Babel project, developing robust speech processing technology for low-resource languages.

I recieved my M.S. from the University of Edinburgh's Speech and Language Processing programme. My thesis, supervised by Ewan Klein, involved adding an open-domain semantic parser to NLTK using CCG and Neo-Davidsonian event semantics.
I completed my B.A. in Philosophy at Loyola University of Chicago. My honors thesis was titled "The Word of Speech". In it I discussed the theory of speech introduced by Merleau-Ponty in which the speech act is analogous to bodily interaction with the world.

I am also an avid cook and baker, artist, gardener, and Zen buddhist.
